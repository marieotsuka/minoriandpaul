Our Story

A little history of our relationship.

  • Growing Up

    Paul and Minori lived on Glezen lane growing up as two kids — a boy obsessed with his army gear and a girl who barely spoke English. They both attended Red Barn School for pre-school, at which Paul has a memory of themselves playing on the swing set together as their mothers talked.

  • Highschool

    Paul and Minori spent High school in different circles. Minori was the nerdy girl who taught piano and hung out with the AP math teachers, while Paul spent his time baking pies and playing guitar.

  • College

    Once they graduated Wayland High, Paul could not wait to get out of the town and went to USC in Los Angeles. Minori stayed close to her family and her close knit friends from Wayland and attended Boston University. They both turned out to be nerds, (surprise!) and went on to get a degree in Engineering.

  • A surprise reunion

    Minori and Paul both attended yet another neighbor, John Jaques’ wedding where Paul swears Minori couldn’t resist him; Minori disagrees. John (read: Thutrang) decided to seat them at the same table in the hopes of kindling a romance. Who would have thought it actually worked!

  • Fitness Partners

    They started dating and connected over there ridiculous goals of Minori’s marathon and Paul’s Half Ironman. They found fitness to be a perfect way to bond (and allow themselves to eat more).

  • World Travelers

    As things got more serious, they shared their love of traveling. Their first trip was a Christmas in NYC, and their second was a journey to Tokyo, where Paul met Minori’s grandparents.

  • Engagement

    At their most recent NYC trip, Paul proposed to Minori at Central Park. Since you’re reading our wedding website, it’s safe to assume she said “Yes.” Come join us for the next moment in our timeline on April 22nd at the Andover Country Club.

Our Proposal

Behind the engagement.

Minori and Paul have planned a trip to NYC every Christmas, so Paul knew that an NYC proposal would be the best possible present. The where came easy, but the “how” was difficult.

First he had to ask her parent’s permission — a custom not ordinarily done in Japan. It took a little convincing for them to understand why Paul insisted that he picked up their dog, Sheila, in person at 8:00 am before a trip. Kenji, her father, was equally confused as to why such an occasion necessitated him getting out of bed. But he did, and Paul explained that their daughter made him the happiest man on earth. He showed them the ring and they knew it was serious. He kept debating how to do it. Rockerfeller Center? There is a great Christmas Tree, but crazy crowds. Bryant Park? A lesser Christmas Tree, but lesser crowds as well.

Upon approaching NYC Paul realized the folly of these plans. They both required him to wait until nightfall, and Paul has never been known for the virtue of patience. So they began walking around the city, and Paul decided that they should visit the Peace Memorial in Central Park. Minori was confused by how antsy Paul was being all day, but she went with it. They began walking, and Paul decided to tell a story for Minori.

He told how once upon a time, they met as children. They reconnected at a wedding and fell hopelessly in love. Minori insisted that the “in love” part happened much later, but Paul was convinced it was on the same night. As the story meandered, much like this one, Minori was confused why they seemed to be endlessly wandering. The confusion came to a head when Paul insisted that they stop for a second on a scenic rock overlooking the ice rink.

He said that he hoped their story ended with a happily ever after and as he got down on one knee he asked her if she could be the person to make that happen. She started crying (despite swearing it would never happen), Paul got excited to hug her and got off his knee too early. They spent the next hour wandering around New York on cloud 9 — an experience that won’t be exceeded until this April.
